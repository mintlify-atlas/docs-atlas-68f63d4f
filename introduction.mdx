SlideMenu is a SwiftUI component that adds a native-feeling slide-out side menu to any iOS app. It supports left and right edges, drag gestures with axis locking, tap-to-dismiss, and complete appearance control through view modifiers — all with no external dependencies.

## What SlideMenu does

SlideMenu wraps your app's content in a two-panel container. The main content panel slides aside to reveal the menu panel beneath, driven by edge swipe gestures or a programmatic toggle. The whole experience feels native because SlideMenu matches the device's display corner radius and uses standard SwiftUI animations.

## How it works

The component uses a `DragGesture` with axis locking to distinguish horizontal swipes from vertical scrolling. This means SlideMenu works correctly alongside `ScrollView`, `List`, and `Form` — vertical scroll gestures are passed through transparently.

Opening and closing the menu can happen three ways:
- **Edge swipe** — drag from the screen edge in the configured zone
- **Tap-to-dismiss** — tap the dimming overlay while the menu is open
- **SlideMenuButton** — a button component that reads the toggle action from the environment automatically
